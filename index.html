⚡Immediate Reaction
Announcement after close. Next morning: stock gaps up 3–7% at open. Arbitrageurs who predicted it take profits. New buyers pile in anticipating passive buying pressure.
📰Market Signal
Pure mechanical signal — no new fundamental information. Price jump reflects anticipated demand from passive funds that must buy.

+3–7% Gap UpVolume Spike
🏃Arbitrageur Activity
Front-runners who bought pre-announcement or at open now sit long. They sell into passive buying on effective date. Active managers also buy to reduce future tracking error vs benchmark.
📖Order Book Dynamics
Ask side thins out — sellers pull offers, expecting higher prices. Bid side deepens as buyers accumulate. Spread may tighten. Continued upward drift as effective date approaches.

Continued Drift UpThin Ask Side
🏦Passive Funds Execute
ETFs and index funds submit Market-On-Close (MOC) buy orders. Must buy regardless of price — zero discretion. All hit simultaneously at 4PM close. Volume can be 10–20× normal daily volume.
💰Price Impact
Closing auction clears at elevated price — wherever supply meets the forced demand. Arbitrageurs who bought earlier sell into this wave. Price hits its peak.

Peak PriceMax VolumeZero Tracking Error
📉Reversion Pattern
Forced buying is complete. No more mechanical demand. Arbitrageurs who sold at close take profits. Price slowly reverts toward fundamental value over 2–4 weeks.
⚖️Fundamental vs. Price
If price was pushed above fundamental value by mechanical buying, reversion is a fade. This is the classic S&P 500 inclusion reversal documented in the literature.

🤖ML Models & Quants
Predict likely exclusions before announcement using deteriorating earnings, market cap drift below threshold, liquidity failures. Build short positions quietly. This is the thesis edge.
📊Stock Status
Stock may show signs of criteria breach: earnings turning negative, float shrinking, market cap drifting below S&P 600 threshold. Still included in index. Normal but potentially already weak order book.

Still In IndexWeakening Fundamentals
⚡Immediate Reaction
Announcement after close. Next morning: gap down at open. Shorts pile in. Market makers immediately pull bid-side liquidity. Spread widens dramatically from ~5¢ to 30–50¢.
⚠️Order Book Shock
Market makers know forced selling is coming — they will not be the buyer. Bids pulled far below mid. Ask side fills with short sellers. Book becomes severely asymmetric.

–3–8% Gap DownSpread Blows OutBids Pulled
📉Selling Cascade
Active managers reduce positions (holding excluded stock increases tracking error). Short interest builds visibly — borrow costs rise from ~0.5% to 5–10%+ annualized, limiting further short accumulation.
📖Order Book Deterioration
Each day, bid depth thins further. Uninformed buyers stay away (adverse selection risk). Stock may trade at 50–80% of normal daily volume but with 2–3× normal price impact per trade.

Continued Drift DownRising Borrow CostThin Bids
🏦Passive Funds Execute
All passive ETFs and index funds submit MOC sell orders simultaneously at 4PM close. For a small-cap this can be 10–20× average daily volume concentrated in seconds. No offsetting Russell buyer (already in Russell 2000).
🕳️Price Trough
Closing auction clears far below fundamental value — liquidity is exhausted. Price overshoots downward. This is the maximum mechanical dislocation between price and intrinsic value.

📈Reversion / Your Trade
Forced selling complete. Shorts cover — this is buying pressure. Market makers return (no more directional risk). Spreads normalize. Value buyers enter if overshoot was severe enough.
💡Trading Strategy Payoff
If exclusion was mechanical (not fundamental collapse), stock reverts toward intrinsic value over 2–4 weeks. Short entered at T-30 covers at trough. Long entered at trough profits from reversion.
